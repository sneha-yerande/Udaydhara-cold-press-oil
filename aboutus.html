About Us

No chemical is used while extracting the oil in the wood press (Lakdi Ghana) machine. The oils retain all their original nutrients and aroma. The wood used in the Lakdi Ghana does not create any heat while pressing the seeds, and thus the nutrients are retained fully in the oil. The extracted oils are stored in containers and remain at rest for 2 days before being packed. So that all the residue is settled and we get pure oil. Even the residue we get is used as cattle feed, and it is very nutritious.

About Ghana

This process was invented by the ancient Rishis of India and has been followed for many centuries. In Tamil, it is called “chekku”, in Telugu “ganuga”, in Kannada “ghani” and in Hindi, it is called “Lakdi Ghana”. It is a traditional method used for oil extraction using wooden churners.
